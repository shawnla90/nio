## Critical (20 rules)

### em_dashes
**Em-dash abuse. Delete all. Use periods, commas, or restructure.** | Action: `replace` | Severity: 3

Bad:
- `I built it — and it worked`
- `The tool — which I use daily — is great`
Good:
- `I built it. It worked.`
- `The tool I use daily is great.`

### en_dashes
**En-dash abuse. Same treatment as em-dashes.** | Action: `replace` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `Monday – Friday`
Good:
- `Monday to Friday`

### authority_signaling
**Authority signaling phrases. Delete the setup, state directly.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 3

Bad:
- `The uncomfortable truth about GTM is...`
- `Let me be clear: this works.`
Good:
- `GTM has a problem...`
- `This works.`

### narrator_setup
**Narrator setup lines. Delete the setup, start with the actual point.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 3

Bad:
- `Here's the thing about Clay tables...`
- `Here's where it gets interesting.`
Good:
- `Clay tables let you...`
- `The scoring model flipped.`

### dramatic_rhetorical
**Dramatic rhetorical framing. State what happened. Let the reader feel it.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `But here's the part where it gets real.`
- `And that's when it clicked.`
Good:
- `The pipeline broke at step 3.`
- `I saw the pattern.`

### three_parallel_sentences
**Three parallel dramatic sentences. One direct statement lands harder. Cut to one.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `You can't see it. You can't copy-paste it away. You have to know it exists.`
Good:
- `You have to know it exists.`

### bookend_summary
**Opening thesis restated at the end. Real thinking goes somewhere new by the end.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `In summary, the pipeline works.`
- `In conclusion, we shipped it.`
Good:
- `The pipeline works. Next: scoring.`
- `We shipped it. Here's what broke.`

### self_branded_concepts
**Self-branded concepts. Just explain it.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `This is what I call the 'signal stack'.`
Good:
- `The signal stack pulls from three sources.`

### artificial_drama
**Artificial drama sentences. Show why it's hard with a specific example.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `The shift sounds simple. It's not.`
Good:
- `The shift took 3 weeks because the schema migration broke twice.`

### colon_lists
**Colon-listed everything. Reads like a PowerPoint slide. Write natural sentences.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `the result: better data. the impact: faster sales.`
Good:
- `Better data meant faster sales.`

### humble_brag
**Humble brag disclaimer. Share your take or don't. The disclaimer makes it worse.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `I don't have all the answers, but here's what worked.`
Good:
- `Here's what worked.`

### hype_words
**Lazy energy words that say nothing. Replace with what actually changed or happened.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 3

Bad:
- `This tool is a game changer.`
- `Unleash your pipeline.`
Good:
- `This tool cut our enrichment time from 4 hours to 20 minutes.`
- `Your pipeline runs itself.`

### no_fluff_disclaimers
**Saying 'no fluff' is itself fluff. If the content has no fluff, the reader will know.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `No fluff, just tactics.`
- `No BS guide to Clay.`
Good:
- `Tactics.`
- `A guide to Clay.`

### nada_filler
**Performative emphasis that reads as AI padding. Cut it.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `No templates, nada.`
- `Nada. Just vibes.`
Good:
- `No templates.`
- `Just vibes.`

### sycophantic_openers
**Sycophantic openers that signal AI-generated responses.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `Great question! Here's how...`
- `Absolutely! The way I see it...`
Good:
- `Here's how...`
- `The way I see it...`

### self_referential_openers
**Opens with YOU instead of VALUE. The reader doesn't care about your process.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `Dropping this resource for anyone who needs it.`
- `Sharing something I built last week.`
Good:
- `Resource: how to score ICP fit in Clay.`
- `I built a scoring model last week. It does X.`

### announcement_without_value
**Announcement-style title with no value proposition.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `My new course is live.`
Good:
- `My new course teaches you to build Clay tables from scratch in 2 hours.`

### false_drama_ellipsis
**Ellipsis used to build false suspense before a common word.** | Action: `replace` | Severity: 2

Bad:
- `I tried everything... and then it worked.`
Good:
- `I tried everything. Then it worked.`

### hedging_transitions
**Overused transition phrases that signal AI-generated structure.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `That said, the tool works.`
- `That being said, I'd still use it.`
Good:
- `The tool works.`
- `I'd still use it.`

### filler_transitions
**Filler transition phrases that add nothing.** | Action: `delete` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `Without further ado, here's the workflow.`
Good:
- `Here's the workflow.`

## Context (5 rules)

### engagement_bait
**Engagement bait endings. Generally avoid, but OK on meme posts.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `So here's my question for you: what's your stack?`
Good:
- `What's your stack? Drop it in the comments.`

### bullets_for_arguments
**Arguments belong in prose. Bullets for execution, prose for reasoning.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `Why this matters:
- It saves time
- It reduces errors
- It scales better`
Good:
- `This saves time because the enrichment runs in parallel. Fewer manual steps means fewer errors. And it scales to 10K rows without changes.`

### false_dichotomies
**False dichotomies. Avoid, but legitimate old-vs-new contrasts are fine.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `It's not luck, it's strategy.`
Good:
- `Strategy compounds. Luck doesn't.`

### bold_headers_as_transitions
**Headers for navigation only. LinkedIn formatting is whitespace-driven.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `**The Problem** Most GTM teams...`
Good:
- `Most GTM teams...`

### quotation_overuse
**Quoted phrases that don't need quotes. Write them as plain text.** | Action: `warn` | Severity: 1

Bad:
- `I call this a "growth loop".`
Good:
- `I call this a growth loop.`
